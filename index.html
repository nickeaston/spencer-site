Questions

The things you're already thinking.

Honest answers to the objections that come up in every first conversation. If yours isn't here, the call is the place to raise it, that's the whole point of the hour.

How is Spencer different from using Claude or ChatGPT myself?

Claude and ChatGPT are raw tools. Powerful, but you still have to know what to build, how to connect it to your calendar, email and data, and how to maintain it when it breaks. Spencer is the system built around those tools, your context, your voice, your history, continually refreshed. You don't prompt-engineer; you just talk. Every conversation picks up where the last one left off.

Is Spencer only for businesses?

No. Spencer is for anyone building something, a company, a side project, a healthier family rhythm, a clearer investing thesis, a calmer week. The same brain works across every corner of your life. Clients range from founders and executives to tradies and parents, the common thread is wanting more leverage, not more noise.

What about my data and privacy?

Your data stays in systems you control, your Google Drive, your email account, your calendar. We don't store your private data on our own servers. Every integration runs against APIs or accounts in your name, authenticated via your credentials. When you stop working with us, we hand you everything, code, configs, guides, and walk away. No lock-in, no data captured.

What happens when it breaks?

Every Spencer deployment includes monitoring. We see failures the moment they happen, usually before the client does. For project engagements, we fix issues within a 30-day support window. For retainer clients, it's continuous. Our own Spencer has a diagnostic heartbeat that alerts us on silent failures; we bake the same into everything we build.

How long until Spencer is useful?

Week 1 you get the core integrations and daily briefings. Month 1 Spencer has learned your rhythms, your people, your projects, your patterns. Month 3 you'll wonder how you worked without it. Every week he sharpens; the relationship compounds.

What if I want to leave? Am I locked in?

You own everything we build. All code, configs, credentials, and guides transfer to you on request, at any point, for any reason. Retainer contracts are month-to-month with 30 days notice. The project tier is one-off; no obligation to continue. This is deliberate, lock-in makes for bad consultants. We stay because the work is valuable, not because you can't leave.

How technical do I need to be?

Not at all. Spencer's clients range from tradies to executives. You need to know your business, we handle the technical side. Every system comes with a plain-language guide written for the person who has to actually use it. If something ever needs your attention, we bring it to you in English, not code.

Is AI actually ready for this?

For the kinds of work Spencer handles, sorting information, drafting documents, monitoring and alerting, summarising, following repeatable processes, yes, unambiguously. For judgement calls that need deep context, relationship-building, or creative vision, AI isn't ready and won't be soon. Our job is knowing which is which. We put AI on the right tasks, leave you free for the parts only you can do.

How does this cost work?

The first conversation is free, a full hour, no pitch, no pressure. Pricing gets agreed after we understand what Spencer would actually be doing for you. We don't believe in off-the-shelf tiers because no two lives or businesses are the same. The first hour is also how you figure out whether there's any fit at all, most conversations end in clarity either way, not a contract.
